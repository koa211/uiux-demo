Quality as a Journey

From artisan workshops to AI-assisted testing, QA has evolved from end-of-line checks to a culture of continuous quality.

Scroll ↓

1) Pre-Industrial Craftsmanship

Era: Before mass production

  • Single artisan builds & inspects the whole product.
  • Quality = reputation; no formal process or roles.

Then: Mastery & pride. Now: Teams & standards.

2) Industrial Revolution

Era: Late 1800s–Early 1900s

  • Production separates workers and inspectors.
  • Scientific Management (Taylor): procedures & measurement.

Shift: From craft to inspection roles.

3) Statistical Quality Control

Era: 1920s–1940s

  • Shewhart introduces SPC: control charts, sampling.
  • WWII: reliability at volume drives method adoption.

Shift: Detecting variation early, not only at the end.

4) Total Quality & Standards

Era: 1950s–1970s

  • Deming & Juran: quality is everyone’s job.
  • Kaizen, quality circles, ISO standards.

Shift: From inspection to continuous improvement.

5) Software Quality Emerges

Era: 1970s–1980s

  • Borrowing from manufacturing; tests after coding.
  • Early IEEE standards for software quality.

Pain: Late defect discovery = costly fixes.

6) Shift Left & Process Models

Era: 1990s–2000s

  • From Waterfall to Spiral/RUP; earlier QA involvement.
  • Code reviews, requirements checks, test management tools.

Shift: Prevention over detection.

7) Agile, CI/CD, Test Automation

Era: 2001–2010s

  • QA integrated into sprints; “Quality Engineering.”
  • CI/CD pipelines; fast regressions; higher coverage automation.

Shift: Continuous quality, not stage gates only.

8) Modern Era: Shift-Left + Shift-Right

Era: 2010s–Now

  • Design-phase QA, observability in prod, feature flags.
  • Visual regression, Playwright/Cypress, AI-assisted testing.
  • UX, accessibility, performance as first-class quality.

Next: Quality as a product competency, owned by the org.
